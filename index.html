Micronutrients: Pros, Cons, and Context

Micronutrient Pros Cons Context
Vitamin A (Retinoids, Beta-Carotene)
  • Essential for vision, especially night vision.
  • Supports immune function.
  • Promotes cell growth.
  • Important for bone growth.
  • Antioxidant (beta-carotene).
  • High doses of retinoids toxic.
  • Birth defects (excess in pregnancy).
  • Toxicity: nausea, headache.
  • Carotenodermia (harmless).
 Found in liver, dairy, eggs (retinoids); orange/yellow fruits & veggies (beta-carotene).
Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)
  • Energy metabolism.
  • Supports nerve function.
  • Heart health.
  • Deficiency: beriberi.
  • Alcohol interferes with absorption.
  • High doses: upset stomach (rare).
 Found in whole grains, pork, legumes. Deficiency more common in populations with high refined grain intake.
Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)
  • Energy production.
  • Cellular function.
  • Antioxidant.
  • Healthy skin, eyes.
  • Deficiency: skin problems.
  • Excess excreted (rare toxicity).
  • Bright yellow urine (harmless).
 Found in milk, eggs, meat, leafy greens. Light sensitive, so store foods appropriately.
Vitamin B3 (Niacin)
  • Energy metabolism.
  • Healthy skin, nerves.
  • Lowers cholesterol (nicotinic acid).
  • Digestive function.
  • High doses: flushing, liver damage.
  • Nausea at high doses.
  • Deficiency: pellagra.
 Found in meat, poultry, fish, whole grains. Nicotinic acid form used for cholesterol management.
Vitamin B5 (Pantothenic Acid)
  • Energy metabolism.
  • Hormone/cholesterol synthesis.
  • Wound healing.
  • Deficiency extremely rare.
  • High doses: mild diarrhea/nausea.
 Widely available in many foods; "pantothenic" means "from everywhere."
Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)
  • Brain development/function.
  • Neurotransmitter/RBC production.
  • Regulates homocysteine.
  • Immune function.
  • High doses: nerve damage.
  • Deficiency: anemia, skin problems.
 Found in meat, fish, poultry, bananas, potatoes. Important for those with certain medical conditions.
Vitamin B7 (Biotin)
  • Metabolism of fats, carbs, protein.
  • Healthy hair, skin, nails.
  • May regulate blood sugar.
  • Deficiency rare: hair loss, rashes.
  • High doses generally safe, but may affect lab tests.
 Found in eggs, nuts, seeds, sweet potatoes. Raw egg whites contain avidin, which binds biotin and prevents absorption.
Vitamin B9 (Folate/Folic Acid)
  • Cell growth/division (pregnancy).
  • Prevents neural tube defects.
  • RBC formation.
  • May reduce cancer risk.
  • High doses mask B12 deficiency.
  • Deficiency: anemia.
 Found in leafy greens, legumes, citrus fruits. Folic acid is the synthetic form used in supplements and fortified foods.
Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)
  • Nerve function, RBC formation.
  • DNA synthesis.
  • Brain health.
  • Deficiency: anemia, nerve damage.
  • Absorption issues in older adults.
  • Vegans/vegetarians at risk.
 Found primarily in animal products (meat, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy). Vegans need supplements or fortified foods.
Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)
  • Antioxidant.
  • Immune function.
  • Collagen synthesis.
  • Enhances iron absorption.
  • May reduce cold duration.
  • High doses: digestive upset.
  • Deficiency: scurvy.
  • May interfere with tests.
 Found in citrus fruits, berries, tomatoes, peppers. Easily destroyed by heat and light.
Vitamin D (Calciferol)
  • Calcium absorption, bone health.
  • Immune function.
  • Chronic disease prevention.
  • Produced by sunlight.
  • High doses toxic: hypercalcemia.
  • Toxicity: nausea, kidney problems.
  • Deficiency: rickets, osteomalacia.
 Found in fatty fish, egg yolks, fortified foods. Sunlight exposure is a major source.
Vitamin E (Tocopherol)
  • Antioxidant.
  • Protects cells.
  • Immune function.
  • Heart disease/cancer prevention.
  • High doses: bleeding risk.
  • Deficiency rare: nerve damage.
 Found in nuts, seeds, vegetable oils, leafy greens. Alpha-tocopherol is the most active form.
Vitamin K (Phylloquinone, Menaquinone)
  • Blood clotting.
  • Bone health.
  • Gut bacteria synthesis.
  • Deficiency: excessive bleeding.
  • Interferes with blood thinners.
  • Newborns get K injection.
 Found in leafy greens (K1), fermented foods, and animal products (K2).
Choline
  • Brain development/function.
  • Liver health.
  • Acetylcholine production.
  • High doses: fishy body odor.
  • Deficiency: liver/muscle damage.
 Found in eggs, meat, fish, soybeans. Important during pregnancy for fetal development.
Calcium
  • Strong bones and teeth.
  • Muscle function.
  • Nerve transmission.
  • Blood clotting.
  • High doses: kidney stones.
  • May interfere with absorption of other minerals.
  • Constipation.
 Found in dairy products, leafy greens, fortified foods. Vitamin D is essential for calcium absorption.
Iron
  • Oxygen transport (hemoglobin).
  • Energy production.
  • Immune function.
  • High doses: toxic, organ damage.
  • Deficiency: anemia.
  • Constipation.
 Found in red meat, poultry, fish, beans, fortified cereals. Heme iron (animal sources) is more easily absorbed than non-heme iron (plant sources). Vitamin C enhances non-heme iron absorption.
Zinc
  • Immune function.
  • Wound healing.
  • Cell growth and division.
  • Taste and smell.
  • High doses: copper deficiency, nausea.
  • Interferes with iron absorption.
  • Impaired immune function.
 Found in oysters, red meat, poultry, beans, nuts.
Magnesium
  • Muscle and nerve function.
  • Blood sugar control.
  • Blood pressure regulation.
  • Bone health.
  • Energy production.
  • High doses (from supplements): diarrhea, nausea.
  • Kidney problems can lead to toxicity.
 Found in leafy greens, nuts, seeds, whole grains, dark chocolate.
Copper
  • Iron metabolism.
  • Connective tissue formation.
  • Nerve function.
  • Energy production.
  • High doses: liver damage, nausea.
  • Deficiency: anemia, neurological problems.
 Found in shellfish, nuts, seeds, whole grains, chocolate.
Iodine
  • Thyroid hormone production.
  • Metabolism regulation.
  • Brain development.
  • High doses: thyroid problems.
  • Deficiency: goiter, hypothyroidism.
 Found in iodized salt, seafood, seaweed, dairy products.
Selenium
  • Antioxidant.
  • Thyroid function.
  • Immune function.
  • High doses: hair loss, nail changes, garlic breath.
  • Deficiency: Keshan disease (heart problem), Kashin-Beck disease (joint problem)
 Found in Brazil nuts, seafood, meat, poultry, eggs.
Manganese
  • Bone formation.
  • Wound healing.
  • Metabolism of carbohydrates, cholesterol, and amino acids.
  • High doses (from industrial exposure): neurological problems.
  • Deficiency: rare.
 Found in whole grains, nuts, leafy vegetables, tea.
Chromium
  • May enhance insulin action.
  • May play a role in blood sugar control.
  • High doses (from supplements): may cause kidney or liver problems.
  • Deficiency: impaired glucose tolerance (rare).
 Found in broccoli, whole grains, meat, brewer's yeast. The role of chromium in blood sugar control is still being researched.
Molybdenum
  • Enzyme cofactor (helps enzymes function).
  • Important for processing sulfur-containing amino acids.
  • High doses: gout-like symptoms (rare).
  • Deficiency: extremely rare.
 Found in legumes, grains, nuts.
Fluoride
  • Strengthens tooth enamel.
  • Prevents tooth decay.
  • High doses: dental fluorosis (mottling of teeth), skeletal fluorosis.
 Found in fluoridated water, toothpaste, tea.
