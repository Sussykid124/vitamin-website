Micronutrients and Supplements: In-Depth Information

Micronutrient/Supplement Pros Cons Context
Vitamin A (Retinoids, Beta-Carotene)
  • Vision: Crucial for rhodopsin production (night vision). Supports cornea health.
  • Immune Function: Maintains epithelial barriers. Regulates T-cell and antibody responses.
  • Cell Growth: Essential for cell differentiation and gene expression.
  • Bone Growth: Influences osteoblast and osteoclast activity.
  • Antioxidant (Beta-Carotene): Neutralizes free radicals, protecting cells.
  • Toxicity (Retinoids): Hypervitaminosis A: liver damage, bone pain, intracranial pressure.
  • Birth Defects: High doses of preformed Vitamin A during pregnancy: teratogenic effects.
  • Acute Toxicity: Nausea, headache, dizziness, blurred vision, incoordination.
  • Carotenodermia: Excess beta-carotene: yellowing of the skin (benign).
  • Interactions: Retinoid medications, cholesterol-lowering drugs, orlistat.
 Retinoids: liver, dairy, eggs. Beta-carotene: orange/yellow fruits & veggies. Fat-soluble, stored in liver.
Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)
  • Energy Metabolism: Coenzyme in carbohydrate metabolism (pyruvate dehydrogenase, alpha-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase).
  • Nerve Function: Nerve impulse transmission and myelin sheath maintenance.
  • Heart Health: Supports proper cardiac muscle function.
  • Deficiency (Beriberi): Dry beriberi (neurological): muscle weakness, neuropathy. Wet beriberi (cardiovascular): edema, heart failure. Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome (alcohol-related).
  • Absorption Interference: Alcohol, diuretics, certain medications.
  • High Doses: Rarely, very high doses can cause upset stomach.
 Whole grains, pork, legumes. Deficiency: high refined grain intake, alcoholism.
Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)
  • Energy Production: Component of FAD and FMN coenzymes, electron transport chain.
  • Cellular Function: Growth, development, metabolism of drugs and fats.
  • Antioxidant: Helps regenerate glutathione.
  • Healthy Tissues: Maintains skin, eyes, mucous membranes.
  • Deficiency (Ariboflavinosis): Cheilosis (cracks at corners of mouth), glossitis (inflamed tongue), seborrheic dermatitis.
  • Toxicity: Rare, excess excreted.
  • Photosensitivity: Bright yellow urine (harmless).
 Milk, eggs, meat, leafy greens. Light sensitive.
Vitamin B3 (Niacin)
  • Energy Metabolism: NAD and NADP coenzymes, vital for redox reactions.
  • Skin/Nerve Health: Maintains integrity of skin and nervous system.
  • Cholesterol: Nicotinic acid lowers LDL/triglycerides, raises HDL.
  • Digestion: Supports healthy digestive function.
  • DNA Repair: DNA repair and cell signaling.
  • Flushing (Nicotinic Acid): Vasodilation: skin flushing, itching, burning.
  • Liver Damage: High doses of sustained-release nicotinic acid: hepatotoxicity.
  • Pellagra: Deficiency: dermatitis, diarrhea, dementia, death (the "4 Ds").
  • Other: Nausea, vomiting, elevated blood sugar, gout.
 Meat, poultry, fish, whole grains. Nicotinic acid for cholesterol; nicotinamide doesn't flush.
Vitamin B5 (Pantothenic Acid)
  • Energy Metabolism: Component of coenzyme A (CoA), fatty acid synthesis/oxidation, Krebs cycle.
  • Hormone/Cholesterol Synthesis: CoA: steroid hormones, cholesterol, acetylcholine synthesis.
  • Wound Healing: May promote wound healing via cell growth/proliferation.
  • Deficiency: Extremely rare, fatigue, headache, numbness, tingling ("burning feet").
  • High Doses: Mild diarrhea or nausea.
 Widely available; "pantothenic" = "from everywhere."
Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)
  • Brain Function: Synthesis of neurotransmitters (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine, GABA).
  • RBC Production: Essential for heme synthesis.
  • Homocysteine Regulation: Converts homocysteine to cysteine, reducing cardiovascular risk.
  • Immune Function: Lymphocyte production and antibody response.
  • Hormone Regulation: Metabolism of steroid hormones.
  • Neuropathy: High doses (>100mg/day): sensory neuropathy (nerve damage).
  • Deficiency: Microcytic anemia, seborrheic dermatitis, cheilosis, glossitis, depression, confusion.
  • Interactions: Levodopa, phenobarbital, phenytoin.
 Meat, fish, poultry, bananas, potatoes. Important for those with certain medical conditions.
Vitamin B7 (Biotin)
  • Metabolism: Cofactor for carboxylase enzymes: fatty acid synthesis, gluconeogenesis, amino acid metabolism.
  • Hair, Skin, Nails: May improve brittle nails and hair health (limited evidence).
  • Blood Sugar: May help regulate blood sugar in diabetes (some evidence).
  • Gene Expression: Gene regulation and DNA replication.
  • Deficiency: Rare: hair loss, skin rashes (eyes, nose, mouth), brittle nails, neurological symptoms (depression, lethargy, hallucinations).
  • Lab Test Interference: High doses can interfere with certain lab tests.
  • Raw Egg Whites: Contain avidin, binds biotin, prevents absorption.
Eggs, nuts, seeds, sweet potatoes.
Vitamin B9 (Folate/Folic Acid)
  • Cell Growth/Division: DNA/RNA synthesis, crucial during rapid growth (pregnancy, infancy).
  • Neural Tube Defects: Adequate folate intake before/during early pregnancy prevents neural tube defects (spina bifida, anencephaly).
  • RBC Formation: Works with B12 to form RBCs, prevent megaloblastic anemia.
  • Cancer Risk: May play a role in preventing certain cancers (complex relationship).
  • Homocysteine Metabolism: Converts homocysteine to methionine.
  • Masking B12 Deficiency: High folic acid can mask B12 deficiency neurological symptoms, leading to irreversible nerve damage.
  • Deficiency: Megaloblastic anemia, fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath, neural tube defects.
  • Interactions: Methotrexate, phenytoin, sulfasalazine.
 Leafy greens, legumes, citrus. Folic acid is synthetic, used in supplements/fortified foods.
Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)
  • Nerve Function: Myelin sheath formation and nerve impulse transmission.
  • RBC Formation: Works with folate to form RBCs, prevent megaloblastic anemia.
  • DNA Synthesis: Required for DNA synthesis and cell division.
  • Brain Health: May play a role in cognitive function/preventing neurological disorders.
  • Deficiency: Pernicious anemia (lack of intrinsic factor), megaloblastic anemia, neurological problems (numbness, tingling, memory loss, dementia), fatigue, weakness.
  • Absorption Issues: Requires intrinsic factor (stomach). Older adults, pernicious anemia, gastrointestinal disorders: impaired absorption.
  • Vegan/Vegetarian Risk: Primarily in animal products.
 Animal products (meat, poultry, fish, eggs, dairy). Vegans need supplements or fortified foods.
Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)
  • Antioxidant: Protects cells from damage by free radicals.
  • Immune Function: Stimulates white blood cell production/function.
  • Collagen Synthesis: Formation of collagen: structural protein in skin, bones, tendons, ligaments.
  • Iron Absorption: Enhances absorption of non-heme iron (plant sources).
  • Wound Healing: Promotes wound healing through collagen synthesis.
  • Digestive Upset: High doses (>2000mg/day): diarrhea, nausea, abdominal cramps.
  • Scurvy: Deficiency: weakness, fatigue, bleeding gums, skin problems, impaired wound healing.
  • Kidney Stones: High doses may increase risk in susceptible individuals.
  • Interactions: May interfere with medical tests (blood glucose) and medications (chemotherapy).
 Citrus fruits, berries, tomatoes, peppers. Easily destroyed by heat and light.
Vitamin D (Calciferol)
  • Calcium Absorption: Essential for absorption of calcium, promoting bone health.
  • Bone Health: Regulates bone mineralization and remodeling.
  • Immune Function: Modulates immune responses, may reduce infection risk.
  • Chronic Disease Prevention: May prevent heart disease, cancer, autoimmune diseases.
  • Muscle Function: Supports muscle strength and function.
  • Hypercalcemia: High doses: hypercalcemia (high blood calcium): nausea, vomiting, weakness, confusion, kidney problems.
  • Toxicity: Excessive intake: hypercalcemia, calcium deposits in soft tissues (kidneys, heart, lungs).
  • Deficiency: Rickets (children), osteomalacia (adults), increased fracture risk.
  • Interactions: Corticosteroids, orlistat, cholestyramine.
 Fatty fish, egg yolks, fortified foods. Sunlight exposure is a major source.
Vitamin E (Tocopherol)
  • Antioxidant: Protects cell membranes from oxidative damage, especially lipid peroxidation.
  • Cell Signaling: Cell signaling and gene expression.
  • Immune Function: May enhance immune responses.
  • Cardiovascular Health: Some studies suggest heart disease prevention (mixed evidence).
  • Bleeding Risk: High doses may increase bleeding risk, especially with anticoagulants.
  • Deficiency: Rare: nerve damage (peripheral neuropathy), muscle weakness, impaired immune function.
  • Interactions: Anticoagulant/antiplatelet medications, vitamin K, some chemotherapy drugs.
 Nuts, seeds, vegetable oils, leafy greens. Alpha-tocopherol is the most active form.
Vitamin K (Phylloquinone, Menaquinone)
  • Blood Clotting: Synthesis of clotting factors (proteins that help blood clot).
  • Bone Health: Activates proteins involved in bone mineralization.
  • Vascular Health: May help prevent calcification of arteries.
  • Deficiency: Rare in adults, excessive bleeding (hemorrhage).
  • Interference with Blood Thinners: Can interfere with anticoagulant medications (warfarin). Consistent intake is important.
  • Newborn Bleeding: Newborns get vitamin K injection to prevent hemorrhagic disease.
 Leafy greens (K1), fermented foods, animal products (K2). Gut bacteria synthesize K2.
Choline
  • Brain Development: Essential for brain development/function, especially fetal development/infancy.
  • Neurotransmitter Synthesis: Precursor to acetylcholine: memory, muscle control, mood.
  • Liver Health: Helps prevent fat buildup in the liver (fatty liver disease).
  • Cell Membrane Structure: Component of phospholipids: cell membrane structure/function.
  • Fishy Body Odor: High doses (>3.5g/day): fishy body odor, sweating, salivation, low blood pressure.
  • Deficiency: Liver damage (non-alcoholic fatty liver disease), muscle damage, neurological problems.
  • Gastrointestinal Distress: High doses: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea.
 Eggs, meat, fish, soybeans, cruciferous vegetables. Important during pregnancy.
Calcium
  • Bone/Teeth Strength: Major structural component of bones and teeth.
  • Muscle Function: Essential for muscle contraction and relaxation.
  • Nerve Transmission: Nerve impulse transmission.
  • Blood Clotting: Required for activation of clotting factors.
  • Enzyme Activation: Cofactor for many enzymes.
  • Kidney Stones: High intake (especially supplements): increase risk of kidney stones.
  • Mineral Absorption Interference: High intake can interfere with iron, zinc, magnesium absorption.
  • Constipation: High doses can cause constipation.
  • Hypercalcemia: Rare, certain medical conditions or excessive supplement use.
 Dairy products, leafy greens, fortified foods. Vitamin D is essential for calcium absorption.
Iron
  • Oxygen Transport: Component of hemoglobin (red blood cells) and myoglobin (muscle cells): carry oxygen.
  • Energy Production: Electron transport chain, key process in energy production.
  • Immune Function: Supports immune cell function.
  • Cognitive Function: Brain development and cognitive function.
  • Toxicity: High doses: toxic, iron overload (hemochromatosis), damage liver, heart, organs.
  • Deficiency (Anemia): Iron-deficiency anemia: fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath, pale skin, impaired cognitive function.
  • Constipation: Iron supplements can cause constipation.
  • Gastrointestinal Upset: Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain.
  • Interactions: Can interfere with absorption of certain medications.
 Red meat, poultry, fish (heme iron - more easily absorbed). Beans, lentils, spinach, fortified cereals (non-heme iron). Vitamin C enhances non-heme iron absorption.
Zinc
  • Immune Function: Development/function of immune cells.
  • Wound Healing: Cell growth/division, crucial for wound healing.
  • Cell Growth/Division: DNA synthesis and cell division.
  • Taste and Smell: Important for senses of taste and smell.
  • Antioxidant: Helps protect cells from damage.
  • Copper Deficiency: High zinc intake interferes with copper absorption: copper deficiency.
  • Nausea/Vomiting: High doses: nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain.
  • Impaired Immune Function: Excessive zinc can impair immune function.
  • Reduced HDL Cholesterol: High doses can lower HDL ("good") cholesterol.
  • Interactions: Can interfere with absorption of certain medications.
 Oysters, red meat, poultry, beans, nuts.
Magnesium
  • Muscle/Nerve Function: Muscle contraction/relaxation, nerve impulse transmission.
  • Blood Sugar Control: Insulin sensitivity and glucose metabolism.
  • Blood Pressure Regulation: Helps regulate blood pressure.
  • Bone Health: Contributes to bone structure and density.
  • Energy Production: ATP production (energy currency of the cell).
  • Enzyme Function: Cofactor for over 300 enzymes.
  • Diarrhea/Nausea: High doses (from supplements): diarrhea, nausea, abdominal cramping.
  • Toxicity (Kidney Problems): Kidney problems: risk of magnesium toxicity: muscle weakness, difficulty breathing, irregular heartbeat.
  • Interactions: Diuretics, antibiotics, proton pump inhibitors.
 Leafy greens, nuts, seeds, whole grains, dark chocolate.
Copper
  • Iron Metabolism: Absorption, transport, and utilization of iron.
  • Connective Tissue Formation: Component of enzymes: collagen and elastin synthesis.
  • Nerve Function: Maintaining the myelin sheath around nerve fibers.
  • Energy Production: Component of cytochrome c oxidase, enzyme in electron transport chain.
  • Antioxidant: Component of superoxide dismutase (SOD), antioxidant enzyme.
  • Toxicity: High doses: liver damage, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea. Wilson's disease: genetic disorder, copper accumulates.
  • Deficiency: Anemia (impaired iron metabolism), neutropenia (low white blood cell count), neurological problems, bone abnormalities.
  • Interactions: High zinc intake can interfere with copper absorption.
 Shellfish, nuts, seeds, whole grains, chocolate.
Iodine
  • Thyroid Hormone Production: Essential component of thyroid hormones (T3 and T4), regulate metabolism.
  • Metabolism Regulation: Thyroid hormones control metabolism: energy production, growth, development.
  • Brain Development: Crucial for brain development/cognitive function, especially fetal development/infancy.
  • Thyroid Problems: Iodine deficiency and excess: thyroid dysfunction.
  • Goiter: Iodine deficiency: goiter (enlargement of thyroid gland).
  • Hypothyroidism: Iodine deficiency: hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid): fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, cognitive impairment.
  • Hyperthyroidism: Excessive iodine: hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid): weight loss, rapid heartbeat, anxiety, tremors.
  • Interactions: Antithyroid drugs.
 Iodized salt, seafood, seaweed, dairy products.
Selenium
  • Antioxidant: Component of selenoproteins: antioxidant properties, protect cells.
  • Thyroid Function: Conversion of T4 (inactive thyroid hormone) to T3 (active thyroid hormone).
  • Immune Function: Supports immune cell function, may enhance immune responses.
  • Reproduction: Important for male fertility.
  • Toxicity (Selenosis): High doses: hair loss, nail changes, garlic breath, fatigue, irritability, nerve damage.
  • Deficiency: Keshan disease (cardiomyopathy), Kashin-Beck disease (osteoarthritis), impaired immune function.
  • Interactions: Cisplatin and clozapine.
 Brazil nuts, seafood, meat, poultry, eggs. Soil selenium content varies geographically.
Manganese
  • Bone Formation: Bone development and maintenance.
  • Wound Healing: Collagen formation and wound healing.
  • Metabolism: Cofactor for enzymes: metabolism of carbohydrates, cholesterol, amino acids.
  • Antioxidant: Component of manganese superoxide dismutase (MnSOD), antioxidant enzyme.
  • Toxicity (Manganism): High doses (industrial exposure to manganese dust): neurological problems similar to Parkinson's disease.
  • Deficiency: Rare in humans, impair growth, bone formation, glucose tolerance.
  • Interactions: Iron and calcium can decrease manganese absorption.
 Whole grains, nuts, leafy vegetables, tea.
Chromium
  • Insulin Action: May enhance action of insulin, regulates blood sugar.
  • Blood Sugar Control: Some studies suggest chromium supplementation may improve blood sugar control in diabetes (mixed evidence, more research needed).
  • Lipid Metabolism: May play a role in lipid metabolism.
  • Toxicity: High doses (from supplements): kidney or liver problems, skin reactions. Hexavalent chromium (Cr(VI)) is a carcinogen, not in food/supplements.
  • Deficiency: Impaired glucose tolerance (rare).
  • Interactions: Antacids, corticosteroids, insulin.
  • Poor Absorption: Chromium is poorly absorbed.
 Broccoli, whole grains, meat, brewer's yeast. Role of chromium in blood sugar control is controversial.
Molybdenum
  • Enzyme Cofactor: Essential cofactor for several enzymes: sulfite oxidase, xanthine oxidase, aldehyde oxidase.
  • Sulfur Metabolism: Sulfite oxidase is crucial for metabolism of sulfur-containing amino acids.
  • Detoxification: Xanthine oxidase and aldehyde oxidase: detoxification of certain compounds.
  • Toxicity: High doses can cause gout-like symptoms (rare).
  • Deficiency: Extremely rare in humans, neurological problems and sulfite sensitivity.
  • Interactions: High molybdenum intake can interfere with copper absorption.
 Legumes, grains, nuts.
Fluoride
  • Strengthens Enamel: Incorporates into tooth enamel, more resistant to acid attack from bacteria.
  • Prevents Decay: Reduces risk of dental caries (cavities).
  • Remineralization: Promotes remineralization of early tooth decay.
  • Dental Fluorosis: Excessive fluoride intake during tooth development: dental fluorosis (mottling/staining of teeth).
  • Skeletal Fluorosis: Very high fluoride intake over long periods: skeletal fluorosis (bone disease).
  • Acute Toxicity: Very high doses can be acutely toxic: nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, death (rare).
 Fluoridated water, toothpaste, tea. Topical fluoride (toothpaste) is most effective.
Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA, DHA)
  • Heart Health: Reduce triglycerides, lower blood pressure, reduce inflammation, may prevent arrhythmias.
  • Brain Health: Brain development/function, improve cognitive function, reduce dementia risk.
  • Eye Health: DHA: major structural component of retina, protect against age-related macular degeneration.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Reduce inflammation throughout the body.
  • Mental Health: May improve symptoms of depression and anxiety.
  • Fishy Aftertaste: Common side effect of fish oil supplements.
  • Gastrointestinal Upset: Nausea, diarrhea, or heartburn.
  • Bleeding Risk: High doses may increase bleeding risk, especially with blood thinners.
  • Interactions: Anticoagulant and antiplatelet medications.
 Fatty fish (salmon, tuna, mackerel), flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts. EPA/DHA: primarily in fish; ALA (alpha-linolenic acid): plant sources, converted to EPA/DHA (limited conversion).
Coenzyme Q10 (CoQ10)
  • Energy Production: Vital role in electron transport chain, essential for ATP production.
  • Antioxidant: Protects cells from oxidative damage.
  • Heart Health: May improve symptoms of heart failure and reduce blood pressure.
  • Statin-Induced Myopathy: May help reduce muscle pain associated with statin use.
  • Neuroprotection: May have neuroprotective effects.
  • Mild Side Effects: Generally well-tolerated, mild gastrointestinal upset (nausea, diarrhea).
  • Interactions: Blood pressure medications, blood thinners, chemotherapy drugs.
 Organ meats (heart, liver, kidney), beef, poultry, fish, some vegetable oils. Levels decline with age/certain medical conditions.
Probiotics
  • Gut Health: Maintain a healthy balance of gut bacteria, improving digestion/nutrient absorption.
  • Immune Function: Support immune function, modulating gut microbiome.
  • Diarrhea Prevention/Treatment: Can prevent/treat antibiotic-associated diarrhea and traveler's diarrhea.
  • Mental Health: Emerging research: link between gut health and mental health (gut-brain axis).
  • Other: May help with irritable bowel syndrome (IBS), inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), eczema.
  • Mild Side Effects: Generally safe, gas, bloating, or mild digestive upset.
  • Infections (Rare): Rare cases, probiotics can cause infections in people with weakened immune systems.
  • Strain Specificity: Benefits of probiotics are strain-specific: different strains have different effects.
 Found in fermented foods (yogurt, kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi) and dietary supplements. Different strains have different effects, so choosing the right probiotic is important.
